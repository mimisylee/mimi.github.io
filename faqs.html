Little Penguin FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions)

No Question Answer
1 How Many Eggs Does a Little Penguin Lay? Little penguins lay between one and four eggs per clutch. Scientists have observed little penguins laying more than one clutch per breeding season, but it happens very rarely.
2 What Size Is a Little Penguin? As their name implies, little penguins are the smallest penguin species. They weigh about 3.3 pounds; in terms of height, they reach 13 inches â€” just a little bit taller than a school ruler.
3 Is the Little Penguin Endangered? As a whole, little penguins are not endangered. However, individual populations are. In New Zealand, the mainland population has decreased by about 70 percent, and a handful of populations have gone extinct.
