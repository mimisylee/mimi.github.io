Nine Fascinating Little Penguin Facts

  1. Johann Reinhold Forster first described these animals in 1871.
  2. The little penguins around Summerland Beach on Phillip Island do nightly parades, and tourists come to watch.
  3. The University of Tasmania devotes considerable resources to little penguin fact-finding projects.
  4. Hunters valued fairy penguins for their skins and feathers in the 1800s and early 1900s.
  5. In days of old, shipwrecked sailors in the Antipodes ate these animals to survive.
  6. The eggs of these animals were once considered a delicacy, especially among Aboriginal Australians.
  7. The Linux penguin mascot was inspired by a little penguin who pecked the operating systemâ€™s creator during an Australian vacation.
  8. Scientists believe that some fairy penguin populations may occasionally share nesting colonies with seabirds, like prions and short-tailed shearwaters.
  9. Fishers once used fairy penguins to catch southern rock lobsters.
